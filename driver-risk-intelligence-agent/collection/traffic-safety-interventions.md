# Traffic Safety Interventions — Playbook (Sample)

Match the intervention to the **dominant risk driver**, scaled to the risk band.

## By risk band
- **Low (0–39):** Maintain. Annual refresher; positive reinforcement; no restriction.
- **Medium (40–69):** Targeted coaching on the specific behaviour; 30-day re-score to confirm improvement.
- **High (70–100):** Immediate review with supervisor; structured coaching plan; consider temporary route/time adjustments until the score improves.

## By dominant risk driver
### High-speed offenders (speeding events dominate)
- In-cab speed alerts / intelligent speed assistance.
- One-to-one coaching reviewing specific events.
- Where persistent, escalate per the disciplinary policy.

### Distraction (phone use dominates)
- Enforce hands-free / do-not-disturb-while-driving policy.
- Education on distraction risk; remove pressure to answer while driving.

### Harsh braking / acceleration (smoothness)
- Eco/defensive-driving coaching on following distance and anticipation.
- Check whether specific routes or schedules force harsh manoeuvres.

### Night-time exposure (high NIGHT_KM_PCT)
- Review rostering to reduce sustained night driving.
- Fatigue-management briefing; ensure adequate rest breaks.

### Fatigue (fatigue flags)
- Enforce hours-of-service and break rules.
- Investigate scheduling; consider journey-management planning.

## Programme-level actions
- Run awareness campaigns on the most common authority-wide risk driver each quarter.
- Recognise and reward sustained Low-band drivers.
- Track whether interventions actually lower the next-period score; drop what doesn't work.

## Principles
- **Proportionate:** match intensity to risk; don't over-restrict low-risk drivers.
- **Behavioural, not personal:** focus on the action and how to change it.
- **Evidence-led:** every recommendation should trace back to a measured risk driver.
