# The Saudi Procurement Framework — Bootcamp Notes

## The instruments
- **Government Tenders and Procurement Law (GTPL)** — Royal Decree No. M/128
  (1440H / 2019), in force since late 2019. Governs how government entities
  tender, award, and manage contracts.
- **Executive Regulations of the GTPL** — the implementing rules: procedures,
  time limits, thresholds, evaluation and award mechanics.
- **Etimad** — the unified government procurement platform operated by NCGR,
  through which tenders, awards, contracts, and payments flow.

## Principles the law establishes (why red flags matter)
- **Competition as the default.** Public tender is the norm; restricted
  methods (limited tenders, direct purchase) are exceptions with conditions —
  which is why repeated direct awards and single-bid patterns warrant review.
- **Transparency and equal treatment** — advertising requirements, published
  award results, and standardized documents; unusually short tender windows
  cut against this.
- **Value for money** — evaluation criteria set in advance; awards far from
  estimates warrant review.
- **Integrity provisions** — conflict-of-interest rules and prohibitions on
  splitting procurements to evade thresholds or approval levels.

## How the agent should use the law
1. Quantify the pattern in the data first (the tools).
2. Retrieve the specific article/regulation from the uploaded PDFs.
3. State: pattern → the rule it implicates → recommended review step.
4. Quote obligations verbatim when they matter ("shall", "may not"), with the
   article number.

## Caution

cited as the law. Where the notes and the uploaded official texts differ,
the official texts win. Legal interpretation belongs to the competent
