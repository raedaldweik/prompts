# RTA Procurement Procedure (Sample)

**Document ID:** PRO-PROC-002 · **Version:** 2.1 · **Owner:** Procurement Department · **Effective:** 2026-03-01

## 1. Purpose
To ensure RTA purchases of goods and services are fair, transparent, competitive, and deliver value for money.

## 2. Thresholds & Required Method
| Purchase value (AED) | Required method |
|----------------------|-----------------|
| Up to 50,000 | Direct purchase with one quotation |
| 50,001 – 500,000 | At least three written quotations |
| 500,001 – 5,000,000 | Formal Request for Proposal (RFP) / tender |
| Above 5,000,000 | Formal tender with Tender Committee approval |

## 3. Single-Source (Sole-Source) Purchases
3.1 A single-source purchase (no competition) is permitted **only** when justified — e.g. a sole authorized supplier, an emergency, or compatibility with existing systems.
3.2 A single-source justification form **must** be approved by the Procurement Manager before commitment, regardless of value.

## 4. Procurement Steps (Standard Tender)
1. Department raises a purchase requisition with approved budget.
2. Procurement prepares the RFP and evaluation criteria.
3. RFP is published; suppliers submit sealed bids by the deadline.
4. An evaluation committee scores bids on technical and commercial criteria.
5. Award recommendation is approved by the appropriate authority per the thresholds above.
6. Contract is signed before any work begins.

## 5. Conflict of Interest
5.1 Anyone involved in evaluation **must** declare any conflict of interest and recuse themselves.
5.2 Splitting a purchase to stay under a threshold is prohibited.

## 6. Emergency Procurement
6.1 In a genuine emergency affecting safety or continuity of service, expedited purchase is allowed with retrospective documentation approved within five working days.

## 7. Records
All procurement decisions, quotations, and approvals **shall** be retained for at least seven years.
