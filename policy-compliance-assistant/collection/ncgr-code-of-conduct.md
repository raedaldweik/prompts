# Code of Conduct (Internal) — Procurement-Relevant Extract

## §1 Integrity
- §1.1 Employees act impartially and in the public interest in every
  procurement decision.
- §1.2 Employees **must not** solicit or accept gifts, hospitality, or any
  benefit from current or prospective suppliers. Promotional items of
  negligible value (below SAR 200) may be accepted once per year per supplier
  and must be declared.
- §1.3 Offering, promising, or accepting a bribe is prohibited and will be
  referred to the competent authorities.

## §2 Conflicts of interest
- §2.1 Employees declare any interest — financial, family, or personal — in a
  supplier participating in a procurement they touch.
- §2.2 A declared conflict removes the employee from the evaluation,
  approval, or oversight of that procurement.
- §2.3 Post-employment: for 12 months, former employees may not represent a
  supplier in procurements they previously oversaw.

## §3 Confidentiality
- §3.1 Bid contents, estimates, and evaluation deliberations are confidential
  (see Data Governance Policy §4.2).
- §3.2 Employees must not use confidential procurement information for
  personal benefit or pass it to any unauthorized person.

## §4 Reporting
- §4.1 Employees report suspected fraud, corruption, or policy breaches
  through the whistleblowing channel; reports may be anonymous.
- §4.2 Retaliation against a good-faith reporter is itself a violation.
