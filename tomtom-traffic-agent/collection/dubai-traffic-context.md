# Dubai / RTA Traffic Context (Sample)

Background the agent can use to interpret live data and give better advice.

## Major corridors
- **Sheikh Zayed Road (E11):** the primary north–south spine linking Dubai and Abu Dhabi; heavy peak congestion around interchanges (Trade Centre, Financial Centre, Al Barsha).
- **Al Khail Road (E44):** parallel alternative to E11, often faster during SZR peaks.
- **Emirates Road (E611) & Sheikh Mohammed Bin Zayed Road (E311):** outer ring routes good for bypassing the city centre and for freight.
- **Dubai–Al Ain Road (E66) and Jebel Ali–Lehbab (E77):** key inland corridors.

## Common bottlenecks
- SZR interchanges during morning (07:00–09:30) and evening (17:00–20:00) peaks.
- Approaches to Dubai International Airport (DXB) terminals at peak travel times.
- Crossings between Bur Dubai and Deira (Al Maktoum / Al Garhoud / Floating bridges and the tunnel).
- Business Bay and Downtown during events at Dubai Mall / Coca-Cola Arena.

## Peak periods (typical)
- **Morning peak:** ~07:00–09:30 (Sun–Thu).
- **Evening peak:** ~17:00–20:00 (Sun–Thu).
- **Weekend:** Sat/Sun leisure peaks near malls, beaches, and attractions.
- Friday afternoons differ from weekday patterns.

## Advice patterns
- During SZR peaks, **Al Khail Road** is often the better alternative — compare live ETAs.
- For airport runs at peak, **add buffer** beyond the raw ETA.
- Outer ring roads (E611/E311) usually beat the centre for cross-city trips.
- Always prefer the **live, traffic-aware ETA** over distance — the shortest route is frequently not the fastest.

> Conditions change constantly; treat this as priors, not facts. The live TomTom
> data is the source of truth for any specific answer.
